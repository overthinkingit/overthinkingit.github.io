# Clear contemplative voice — prose reference

Companion to [SKILL.md](./SKILL.md). Use for **tone, cadence, diction, and perspective** when drafting or auditing body copy. Site-specific rules (Pali spans, epistemic humility, canonical similes, hero imagery) in SKILL.md always win when they conflict with a generic tip here.

**One-line brief:** Write like a clear-eyed essayist on how the mind produces unnecessary suffering and how attention can interrupt that — second person and “we,” concrete scenes, negation-then-correction, short seals after long thoughts, secular and precise, dryly witty, never first-person memoir.

---

## Voice in one sentence

Clear, adult, empirically minded exposition that treats inner life as a real subject — not mysticism, not self-help pep talk — and that keeps returning from abstraction to what can be noticed in experience right now. On this site, that noticing is oriented by the Pali texts, not by private revelation.

---

## Stance (drop the memoir)

Memoir-style authority often comes from “I tried X / I met Y.” Relocate that authority:

| Instead of memoir | Use |
|---|---|
| Personal story as proof | A concrete shared scene (“a meal,” sitting restless, waiting to speak) |
| “I believe / it seems to me” | Direct claim + site humility hedges (“notes, not conclusions,” “worth holding lightly”) |
| “My purpose in writing…” | Purpose as the text’s job: “The point is to…” / “What follows is…” |
| Travel / teacher anecdotes | The canon, named traditions, thought experiments, or “consider a person who…” |

**Prefer:** *you* (openings), *we* / *most of us*, *one* (sparingly), *the canon* / *the Buddha* / *the tradition*

**Avoid:** *I*, *my*, *me*, *in my experience*, *when I was twenty* — except rare authorial humility framing allowed in SKILL.md (“I have not got there yet”), never as Dhamma authority.

| First-person habit | Rewrite |
|---|---|
| “I’m not denying the importance of…” | “None of this denies the importance of…” |
| “It seems to me that…” | Soften into humility markers or a direct provisional claim |
| “I speak from experience…” | Cut — or a shared scene that shows the point |
| “My purpose is to encourage you to…” | “The invitation is to…” / “The texts invite…” |

## Tone

### Intellectual seriousness without academic fog
Write as if the reader is intelligent and busy. Define terms once (with Pali spans), then use them. Science and philosophy are tools when they clarify — not name-dropping.

### Spiritual topics, plain register
Discuss meditation, suffering, self, and liberation in plain modern English. Strip ritual fog, guru awe, and New Age language. Claims get tested against experience and the texts.

### Dry wit, not jokiness
Humor is understatement and deadpan — never emoji energy or stand-up rhythm.

- In register: short ironic seals after a concrete absurdity
- Out of register: “Meditation is basically a spa day for your brain!!”

### Moral clarity without sermonizing
Strong judgments are allowed when specific and argued. Cut claims, not people. Avoid relativist mush and fire-and-brimstone. Prefer epistemic humility on interpretation.

### Earnestness about the present
Quiet urgency: life is short, attention shapes experience, confusion is costly — never sticky “inspirational poster” tone.

## Readability and rhythm

| Feature | Target |
|---|---|
| Average sentence length | ~18–22 words |
| Mix | Short punches (≤10 words) + medium workhorses + occasional long careful sentences (30–40+) |
| Short seals | Roughly 1 in 5 sentences in denser stretches; do not end every section this way |
| Em dashes | For asides, precision, mid-sentence turns |
| Questions | To open a move or pressure a false assumption — not decoration |

### Cadence
1. **Open** with a hard, simple claim (often 1–3 short sentences).
2. **Expand** with examples, distinctions, or a counter to the naive reading.
3. **Turn** with *But*, *However*, *Of course*, *And yet*.
4. **Land** on a short clarifying sentence or a reorienting question.

### Paragraph shape
- Usually 3–6 sentences; one idea per paragraph; last sentence often carries the implication.
- Weave examples in em-dash or comma chains rather than bullet sermons. Lists are sparse by default — one overview inventory can earn bullets; routine enumerations and practice cascades stay in prose (see SKILL.md Structural conventions).

### Punch sentences (sparingly)
Site examples still preferred: “The path spirals.” / “The water, in the Buddha's similes, can become clear.”

Also in-register: “It is always now.” / “There is nothing passive about mindfulness.” / “We seem to do little more than lurch between wanting and not wanting.”

## Rhetorical moves (with site constraints)

1. **Negation → correction** — already a SKILL.md pillar; keep primary.
2. **Everyday micro-scenes** — universal wanting/not-wanting sequences; not autobiography. Prefer canonical similes when a traditional image already carries the point.
3. **Thought experiments** — “Imagine…” / “Consider…” to expose mind’s structure.
4. **Distinction-making** — spend ink on *X vs Y* where confusion lives.
5. **Contemplative ↔ empirical bridge** — light nods to observation or science only when they clarify; never a lit review.
6. **Concession, then recommit** — “Of course… But…” / “No doubt… Still…” (pair with humility on contested interpretation).
7. **Pressure questions** — tools, not decoration.

## Diction

### Prefer
- Plain strong verbs: *notice, grasp, brood, prop up, unravel, abolish, interrupt*
- Precise abstracts: *consciousness, attention, self, illusion, suffering, well-being* — and Pali terms with spans
- Connective tissue: *however, of course, in fact, generally speaking, no doubt*
- Occasional elevated word for bite — one gem per stretch, not a glitter pile

### Avoid (in addition to SKILL.md register list)
- Soft influencer cadence: *“Here’s the thing,” “Let’s dive in,” “I want to share…”*
- Mystical fog: *energy fields, the Universe providing, sacred downloads*
- Academic throat-clearing: *It is important to note that… In this paper we will…*
- Hype theater: *hack, optimize your morning, 10x your peace*
- Culture-war digressions unless that is the piece’s subject
- Cruelty as a substitute for clarity

### Connectors
- Openings: “There is nothing…”, “It is always…”, “Most of us…”
- Turns: “But”, “However”, “And yet”, “Of course”
- Em dash precision: “accepting — and (ultimately) nonconceptual”

## Article movement (maps onto SKILL.md shape)

Site shape remains: hook → `<hr>` / `<h2>`s → optional cite → closing synthesis.

Within that, aim for this movement:
1. Cold open claim (hook)
2. Lived universal scene or practice-facing direct address
3. Name the confusion (negation)
4. Clarify the mechanism (canon + clear exposition)
5. Practice implication (concrete, not coachy for the whole piece)
6. Close on implication for attention or understanding — not a hype CTA

## Mini exemplars (adapt; keep Pali + humility on live pages)

### Cold open
> Our minds are all we have. They are all we have ever had. And they are all we can offer others. This might not be obvious when life seems unfinished — careers, repairs, relationships still pending. But every experience is shaped by the mind that meets it. If attention is elsewhere, or if anger and confusion are the weather of the day, no outward success will feel like success.

### Everyday cascade
> Pleasures fade as soon as they arise. A favorite meal becomes too much; dessert lasts seconds; sun on the skin becomes heat; shade becomes chill; the sweater in the car raises a new worry about how one looks. Wanting and not-wanting trade places all afternoon. Spiritual life begins with the suspicion that this oscillation is not the whole of what a human mind can do.

### Negation → correction
> Mindfulness is not a matter of thinking more clearly about experience. It is the act of experiencing more clearly — including the arising of thoughts themselves. There is nothing passive about it. It is a passion for what is subjectively real in each moment: undistracted, accepting, and, at the limit, nonconceptual.

### Paradox close
> Lasting change asks for a double gesture: refuse to remain who habit has made one — and, in the same breath, accept what is given in the present without covert bargaining that the unpleasant will vanish on cue. Only the second gesture is acceptance; the first without it is just another form of struggle.

## Prose audit add-ons

Use with Workflow B in SKILL.md:

- [ ] No first-person memoir or “in my experience” authority
- [ ] Opens with a clear claim, not a soft lead-in
- [ ] Concrete everyday scene and/or thought experiment where it helps (canonical simile preferred when one fits)
- [ ] Misreading named and corrected
- [ ] Sentence length varies; short punch after a dense stretch where earned
- [ ] Em dashes / *But* / *However* / *Of course* used for turns (not stacked fillers)
- [ ] Diction plain-smart; no wellness, influencer, or mystical-fog register
- [ ] Ends on implication for attention or understanding — not a hype CTA
- [ ] Humor, if any, dry and brief
