Suttas

Discourses

Specific texts from the Pali canon — what they contain, how they work, what they ask of the reader.

A sutta is not a theorem to be proved or a story to be enjoyed — it is, in most cases, a record of a teaching delivered to a particular person in a particular situation. Reading them well means holding that context in mind.

  • SN 56.11 · First discourse

    Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta

    The Discourse Setting the Wheel of the Dhamma in Motion — the Buddha's first teaching after his awakening: the middle way, the four noble truths, and why the first thing he addressed was dukkha rather than metaphysics.

  • MN 10 · Foundations of mindfulness

    Satipaṭṭhāna Sutta

    The Discourse on the Foundations of Mindfulness — the Buddha's most detailed account of sati practice, covering body, feeling-tone, mind states, and phenomena, and described as "the direct path" to liberation.

  • SN 22.59 · Second discourse

    Anattalakkhaṇa Sutta

    The Discourse on the Characteristic of Non-Self — the systematic application of the anattā argument to each of the five aggregates, and what the five listening monks saw at the end of it.

  • AN 3.65 · Free inquiry

    Kālāma Sutta

    The famous discourse on the grounds for belief — what the sutta is actually saying beyond its "don't-believe-it-because" opening, and the brahmavihāra conclusion that is rarely quoted alongside it.
